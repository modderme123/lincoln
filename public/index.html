Abraham Lincoln in
Pop Culture

“Resolve to be honest at all events; and if, in your own judgment, you can not be an honest lawyer, resolve to be honest without being a lawyer. Choose some other occupation.”
- Abraham Lincoln

Abraham Lincoln was thoughtful, strong, kind, independent, and famously tall. These particular characteristics are what captured the interest of filmmakers, novelists, and marketers, some of whose creations are shown in Section 3. Lincoln was a self-made man, whose nickname “Honest Abe,” a nickname inspired by his behavior which only persisted as he grew older.

It seems clear, drawing from historical records and documentation, that Abraham Lincoln believed honesty and integrity to be core to a man's values, and although law, one of Lincoln’s earlier professions, was seen as largely dishonest, Lincoln was quoted saying, “Resolve to be honest at all events; and if, in your own judgment, you can not be an honest lawyer, resolve to be honest without being a lawyer. Choose some other occupation.” This highlights his core values which later served him well as a president and in legacy, which we’ll be focusing on here.

Abraham Lincoln in Advertising

"Everyone wants Lincoln on their side"
- Richard Norton Smith, historian

One of the largest usages of Lincoln is in advertising, as would be expectedーa popular, wise, and trusted authority figure is one of the best ways to market a product. Here are some notable examples:

In a commercial for a sleep aid, Rozerem, Lincoln appears as a wise and knowledgeable figure at the table, sitting with the person suffering from insomnia and a talking beaver. He appears kind and understanding, while still engaging in conversation as if an equal. This is a clear-cut reason why Lincoln is so commonly used in advertisingーpeople believe in what he says, and empathize with him.

Lincoln Logs are now marketed as representative of Abraham Lincoln, although the original history is not clear. Lincoln Logs were the property of Knex, who named them after Lincoln, “who began his celebrated life in a log cabin in Kentucky.” The logs were a success because they were a lot more stable (in terms of construction), and had an appealing title.

The “Honest Abe” deals used for marketing use Lincoln as synonymous with product integrity. This not only shows how Lincoln has impacted pop culture, but also how pop culture has impacted himーthat a deal would be named after him reinforces the idea of his being honest and accountable.

Abraham Lincoln in
Movies and Books

Lincoln has been listed in an immense number of movies, TV shows, novels, and advertisements during and since his death, with an impressive 15,000 books written about him, a number second to only Jesus, according to historian Paul Tetreault. There is a common thread among these portrayals, though: depictions of a hero, one who is both universally revered and relatable. Many advertisements also make use of his famous moniker, “Honest Abe,” to put integrity behind their products. Here, we’ve compiled some portrayals of Lincoln to illustrate these points.

While this newspaper obituary seems very unrelated to presidential legacy, the far-reaching influence of a presidential nickname is shown here. For almost every other president, their name would be used rather plainly, but Lincoln is described with a word with such a positive connotation, part of a widely accepted nickname.

This triumphant song lists Lincoln’s virtuesー”honest, frank, and true.” Although the presence of this song may be an older custom, presidential election songs, perhaps, I can’t recall the last time I heard of a song like this for a president. (Imagine an official Trump campaign song.)

While seemingly exaggeratory, I think this song, like the previous source, highlights the omnipotence and appeal of Lincoln very well.

This song, unlike the previous, seems to focus on Lincoln’s origin and toil, almost to empathize with the listeners. In this way, he is both revered and relatable. Lincoln is put in the same boat as liberty, almost a campaign tactic. However, the near-biblical imagery of his fight against the “Slavocrat’s giant” seems to back the claims about Lincoln. It further emphasizes the good in Lincoln, and is almost a rallying cry.

Lincoln (portrayals of Lincoln) appeared in 297 different films and TV shows from 1911 to 2012, more than Batman, Sherlock Holmes, and James Bond! This is another testament to his appeal, as well as the broad range of interpretation of this president. He appears as both a cool authority figure (Bill & Ted) as well as a vampire hunter (Abraham Lincoln: Vampire Hunter), or famed statesman (Lincoln). Much as with the songs, Lincoln’s level of cinematic portrayal is unique: when was the last time you saw a zombie apocalypse movie starring, say, JFK?

It is thought that Lincoln’s real voice was in fact much higher than his generally low and booming movie portrayal. This is perhaps yet another symbol of how mainstream Lincoln has become, and how pop culture has impacted our perception of him.

Conclusion, Credits, and Sources

Abraham Lincoln’s view in the public eye has evolved over time from a radical anti-slavery candidate for president to the most popular president today. Over time, approval has steadily risen, with assisting quotes like "[e]veryone wants Lincoln on their side" Richard Norton Smith, a presidential historian. To this end, many people and advertising firms have used Lincoln to proclaim a sense of honesty and fairness. In that way, popular culture has likewise molded Lincoln into glory, and often fabricated truths. Lincoln's eternal positivity and pushing for the emancipation of slaves cements him in popular culture as a figure of positivity and light. However during and before his presidency he was not as popular, causing his appearances in popular culture to increase over time. To that end, he has appeared in many movies, more than any other president alive or dead, used extensively in advertising even today, and has been consistently popular though the good and through the bad. In light of all of this, we can see that Lincoln is a beacon of light and positivity, causing him to be used throughout modern media.
